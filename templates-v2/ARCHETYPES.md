# Homepage Archetypes for Local Service Businesses (V2)

## 1) Split-Screen Hero (High-Urgency + Trust)
- **Structural layout:** 2-column hero (copy + proof on left, image/status panel on right), then service tiles and trust bar.
- **Visual style cues:** Dark/high-contrast base, bold accent buttons, real technician imagery, floating “available now” card.
- **Typography approach:** Heavy sans for headlines, legible sans body, compact labels for proof metrics.
- **Navigation pattern:**
  - **Desktop:** horizontal top nav + persistent quote CTA
  - **Mobile:** condensed top bar with primary CTA and anchor jump links
- **Section order:** Hero → Social proof metrics → Services → Reviews → Service area → Contact/footer CTA.
- **CTA strategy:** Dual CTA (“Call now” + “Book online”) at hero and footer; urgency language near availability.
- **Why it converts locally:** Matches emergency/local intent; instantly communicates credibility, speed, and immediate next action.

## 2) Asymmetric Bento Grid (Scan-First Discovery)
- **Structural layout:** Mosaic grid with one dominant statement card and mixed-size utility cards (gallery, ratings, speed, process).
- **Visual style cues:** Card-based, staggered dimensions, high whitespace, modular blocks with varied backgrounds.
- **Typography approach:** Bold geometric sans headlines, concise body copy, numeric metrics emphasized.
- **Navigation pattern:**
  - **Desktop:** compact nav + prominent quote button
  - **Mobile:** stacked content cards with sticky bottom CTA
- **Section order:** Hero card grid → Process steps → Case snippets → Pricing/quote CTA.
- **CTA strategy:** Repeated micro-CTAs inside cards + one strong conversion button (“Get instant quote”).
- **Why it converts locally:** Fast scanning mirrors how local shoppers compare options and trust cues before contacting.

## 3) Editorial Story Layout (Brand + Craft Differentiation)
- **Structural layout:** Article-style hero with serif headline, narrative intro, project stories, process, testimonial quote block.
- **Visual style cues:** Magazine-inspired spacing, muted palette, long-form copy, documentary photography.
- **Typography approach:** Serif display for authority/craft, readable serif or humanist body, subtle uppercase labels.
- **Navigation pattern:**
  - **Desktop:** simple top nav to “stories/method/book”
  - **Mobile:** minimal anchors + clear booking link at story breaks
- **Section order:** Brand story hero → Recent project stories → Method → Testimonial → Consultation CTA.
- **CTA strategy:** Softer high-intent CTA (“Schedule walkthrough”) after trust and narrative are established.
- **Why it converts locally:** Ideal for premium remodel/restoration shops where trust and craftsmanship beat price-first messaging.

## 4) Utility Dashboard Style (Interactive Decision Support)
- **Structural layout:** App-like shell with left rail nav, KPI cards, interactive symptom selector, actionable modules.
- **Visual style cues:** Product UI language, panels, status cards, operational metrics (arrival time, technician availability).
- **Typography approach:** Strong sans hierarchy, compact UI labels, data-forward numeric treatment.
- **Navigation pattern:**
  - **Desktop:** sidebar navigation
  - **Mobile:** condensed top controls with sticky action button
- **Section order:** Value/KPI row → Diagnostic tool → Plan highlights → Dispatch CTA.
- **CTA strategy:** Task-oriented CTA (“Start diagnosis,” then “Book technician”) to reduce friction and uncertainty.
- **Why it converts locally:** Feels modern and useful; users self-qualify quickly and are nudged into booking with confidence.

## 5) Map-First Service-Area Layout (Local SEO + Territory Confidence)
- **Structural layout:** Hero over map or area module, neighborhood chips, nearby jobs timeline, city-page links.
- **Visual style cues:** Geographic anchors, pin icons, route overlays, “currently in your area” badges.
- **Typography approach:** Clean sans with location names emphasized and scannable area lists.
- **Navigation pattern:**
  - **Desktop:** top nav with “Areas Served” mega-dropdown
  - **Mobile:** map toggle + city quick links
- **Section order:** Hero/map → ZIP/neighborhood coverage → Nearby proof/jobs → Services by area → Contact CTA.
- **CTA strategy:** Location-aware CTA (“Check availability in [ZIP]”).
- **Why it converts locally:** Reduces uncertainty about coverage and aligns strongly with “near me” intent.

## 6) Offer-Funnel Landing Style (Promotion / Seasonal Push)
- **Structural layout:** Long-form landing page with offer header, benefits stack, objection handling FAQ, limited-time close.
- **Visual style cues:** Campaign-style ribbons, countdown/expiry language, high contrast offer modules.
- **Typography approach:** Conversion-centric sans, oversized offer numbers, short persuasive blocks.
- **Navigation pattern:**
  - **Desktop:** reduced nav (focus on conversion)
  - **Mobile:** sticky bottom CTA + tap-to-call
- **Section order:** Offer hero → Benefits → Proof/reviews → FAQ/guarantee → Final urgency CTA.
- **CTA strategy:** Single dominant CTA repeated every 1–2 viewport heights; scarcity and risk-reversal messaging.
- **Why it converts locally:** Excellent for lead bursts (spring tune-ups, storm repair seasons, off-peak promotions).
